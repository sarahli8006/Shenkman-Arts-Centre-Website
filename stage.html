Stage Crew




Stage Crew members go through training to be able to fulfill their responsabilities safely. After training, volunteers will initially work as assistants or stagehands. Progression to operators or lead hands is dependent on the volunteer experience and level of training.













Some more information about Stage Crew training and shifts:



Safety is of prime consideration when working on stage and is emphasized in all training sessions. All new volunteers are required to participate in a series of basic training sessions. The first, Backstage 360ยบ, is a required orientation that must be taken prior to the other courses. The remaining introductory courses cover Staging Principles, Introduction to Lighting and Introduction to Audio, and will qualify the volunteer to work as a stagehand and / or assistant. As the Stage Crew member gains experience with the crew, he or she may wish to acquire more specialized knowledge by participating in training sessions on advanced audio and lighting, rigging operation and stage management.



The basic crew commitment is eight hours per month. However, the amount of time a volunteer actually works in a month is dependent on his or her interest and the shows, training and activities scheduled in a month. Crew members are provided with appropriate food and beverages should they work a shift that extends over normal meal periods.










The following are a few specific positions and responsabilities of Stage Crew members:



Audio Board Operator


- Being responsible for all sound components of a production
- Operating the mixing board, CD players, amplifiers and processing equipment
- Setting up and striking microphones, speakers and intercom systems



Lighting Board Operator


- Co-ordinating all lighting aspects using state of the art equipment
- Operating the lighting control board during set-ups, rehearsals and performances
- Supervising and assisting in the hanging and focusing of lighting instruments
- Selecting colours and programing computerized lighting board



Fly Operator


- Overseeing the safe execution of the fly system
- Operating the main curtains and vertical set changes



Stagehand


- Providing production assistance
- Assisting with the unloading and loading of trucks
- Assisting with assembling and striking sets, risers, platforms and other furnishings
- Assisting with scenery changes during rehearsals and performances.



Stage Manager


- Coordinating the staging elements and communications between the technicians and performers



Assistant


- Providing assistance to fly, audio and lighting operators
